Case study — 02

Noor Skincare

A clean-beauty label with a devoted following and a website that turned it away — rebuilt as a brand experience that converts.

Client
Noor Skincare
Sector
Beauty / e-commerce
Year
2026
Services
Identity refresh · UX · Web design
Timeline
10 weeks
01 — The challenge

Noor had done the hard part: a product people reorder and an Instagram audience that behaved like a community. But the website was a rented template wearing the brand like a borrowed coat — generic type, stock layouts, a checkout that leaked at every step. Paid traffic arrived, looked, and left.

The founder was direct: “People love us until they click the link in bio.”

The brief, in one sentence: make the website feel like the product — considered, calm, and worth the price.

02 — The strategy

Clean beauty is a crowded shelf of white bottles and serif whispers. Noor’s difference was ritual — formulations built around Moroccan botanical traditions, made contemporary. We repositioned the digital experience around “the ritual, not the routine”: fewer products per view, slower pacing, ingredient storytelling before price.

The audience insight that drove the architecture: Noor’s buyers research like readers. So the product page was rebuilt as an editorial page that happens to sell.

03 — The process

We prototyped three product-page architectures and tested them against the existing page with real customers. Direction A — a conventional conversion-optimized layout — won on speed and lost on trust. The shipped direction pairs long-form ingredient narrative with a persistent, quiet buy module; slower on paper, stronger where it counts.
