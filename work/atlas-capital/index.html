Case study — 01

Atlas Capital

A boutique investment firm that looked like a boutique — repositioned to win enterprise mandates without losing its edge.

Client
Atlas Capital
Sector
Finance
Year
2025
Services
Strategy · Identity · Guidelines
Timeline
8 weeks
01 — The challenge

Atlas had spent a decade quietly outperforming the market — and looking like it hadn’t. Their identity was assembled over years of urgent decisions: a logo from 2014, a deck template from a departed analyst, a website that apologized for itself. Meanwhile, the firm’s ambition had moved upmarket, into rooms where the first impression is underwritten before the first meeting.

“We keep getting shortlisted with firms half our size,” the founder told us. The work was enterprise-grade. The image was not — and in finance, an incoherent image reads as risk.

The brief, in one sentence: make the outside as institutional as the inside — without becoming beige.

02 — The strategy

We mapped the competitive set on two axes: institutional ↔ entrepreneurial, and generalist ↔ conviction-driven. The big banks own the institutional-generalist corner; the boutiques crowd the entrepreneurial one. The open quadrant — institutional and conviction-driven — was exactly what Atlas already was in practice.

The big idea: “Precision with a spine.” Every design decision would signal institutional discipline first, then reveal a firm opinion at the second glance — a sharpened serif detail, a chart that says what it thinks.

03 — The process

Three directions were explored. Direction B — a geometric monogram system — was the client favorite for a week. We killed it: it photographed beautifully and meant nothing. The final direction grew from the firm’s own vocabulary: the “atlas” as the instrument of navigation, reduced to a single confident meridian line through the wordmark.
