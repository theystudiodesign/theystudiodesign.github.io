Case study — 03

Dar Mimosa

A boutique riad with twelve rooms and no visual voice — given one language for every channel, six weeks before high season.

Client
Dar Mimosa
Sector
Hospitality
Year
2024
Services
Art direction · Content system · Print
Timeline
6 weeks
01 — The challenge

Dar Mimosa is the kind of place guests describe in whispers — twelve rooms around a courtyard, a kitchen worth the flight. Its image told none of that. The Instagram feed mixed phone snapshots with borrowed stock; the booking platforms showed a different property on every channel; the in-room print was laser-printed A4.

High season was six weeks away. There was no time for a full rebrand — and no need for one. What was missing was a voice.

The brief, in one sentence: one visual voice, every channel, before the season starts.

02 — The strategy

Hospitality content fails in one of two directions: the aspirational blur of every luxury hotel, or the desperate collage of every guesthouse. We anchored Dar Mimosa in a third position — “the documented house”: content that behaves like the diary of the place, not its advertisement. Morning light, the same doorway across seasons, the hands that make breakfast.

A strict content grammar made the system executable by the owner’s small team after we left: three shot families, one caption voice, a fixed weekly rhythm.

03 — The process

Week one produced two competing directions. Direction A — warm, saturated, editorial-travel — tested beautifully in isolation and disappeared entirely in a feed of identical riads. We killed it and committed to a quieter register: natural light only, muted tones, negative space that lets the architecture speak.

Two production days on site generated a 120-asset library, tagged by shot family and channel, with layout templates for stories, posts, and the in-room print set.
